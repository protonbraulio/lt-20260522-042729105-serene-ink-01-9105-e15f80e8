## Detailed explanation

To implement lead generation systems best practices, follow these detailed steps:

1. **Define the Owner and Required Inputs**: Clearly identify the owner of the lead generation system and the required inputs from each team. In Berlin, this might involve coordinating with marketing, sales, and customer success teams.

2. **Set the Expected Outcome**: Establish a clear, measurable expected outcome for each lead generated. This could be a qualified lead, a scheduled meeting, or a completed form.

3. **Establish Decision Criteria**: Define the criteria used to qualify or disqualify leads. This helps ensure consistency and prevents leads from slipping through the cracks.

4. **Measure and Optimize**: Regularly review metrics like lead conversion rates, lead response time, and lead quality to identify areas for improvement. Use these metrics to optimize the lead generation system over time.

## Common mistakes

Avoid these common mistakes in lead generation systems:

1. **Unclear Handoffs**: Poorly defined handoffs between teams can lead to confusion, delays, and lost leads. Clearly define handoffs and responsibilities to prevent this.

2. **Inconsistent Criteria**: Inconsistent application of decision criteria can result in poor lead quality. Ensure all teams are using the same criteria to qualify and disqualify leads.

3. **Neglecting Metrics**: Without regular review and optimization of metrics, lead generation systems can stagnate or even deteriorate. Make metrics a regular part of your review process.
