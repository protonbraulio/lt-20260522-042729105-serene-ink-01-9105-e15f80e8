## What is measured

To evaluate lead generation systems effectively, specific metrics and key performance indicators (KPIs) are used. In Berlin, marketing leads should focus on the following:

1. **Lead Conversion Rate**: Measures the percentage of leads that convert into customers. A high conversion rate indicates an effective lead generation system.

2. **Cost per Acquisition (CPA)**: Calculates the total cost of acquiring a new customer. Lower CPA suggests a more efficient lead generation system.

3. **Lead Quality**: Assesses the relevance and suitability of leads generated. High-quality leads result in better conversion rates and reduced sales cycles.

4. **Sales Cycle Length**: Measures the average time it takes for a lead to become a customer. Shorter sales cycles indicate a more streamlined lead generation system.

5. **Return on Investment (ROI)**: Calculates the revenue generated from leads compared to the cost of acquiring those leads. A higher ROI suggests a more profitable lead generation system.

## How to interpret results

Interpreting the results of the lead generation systems evaluation involves comparing the collected data with benchmarks and identifying trends. Here's how to make sense of the results in Berlin:

1. **Trend Analysis**: Look for trends in the data over time. Consistently improving or declining metrics can indicate the effectiveness of recent changes or the need for further improvement.

2. **Benchmarking**: Compare your results with industry benchmarks to understand how your lead generation system performs relative to competitors.

3. **Root Cause Analysis**: When metrics are not improving as expected, perform a root cause analysis to identify the underlying issues and address them effectively.

4. **Cost-Benefit Analysis**: Evaluate the potential return on investment (ROI) of proposed improvements to ensure they are cost-effective and drive meaningful results.
